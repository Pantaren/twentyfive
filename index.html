Ottolenghi

Figge

star

“Ottolenghi” is the most insufferable, self-indulgent fluff I’ve heard in years. Loyle Carner sounds like a man who thinks he's dropping life-changing truths when really he’s just mumbling about a cookbook like he’s the first person to ever sit on a train and feel feelings. Bro, you’re not wise — you’re just sleepy and vaguely middle-class.

The beat is soft jazz wallpaper, and Loyle delivers every line like he’s allergic to charisma. It’s like he’s scared of his own mic. This isn’t introspection — it’s a man drowning in his own tepid thoughts, whispering nonsense and expecting applause.

And don’t think name-dropping Ottolenghi makes you cultured. It makes you sound like you wrote this after buying one overpriced salad and calling it an epiphany.

Loyle, if you’re gonna rap, rap. If you’re gonna do spoken word, at least say something. “Ottolenghi” is a four-minute nap in beige.

Sort it out.
